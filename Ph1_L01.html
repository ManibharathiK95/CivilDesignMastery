Introduction to Civil Design

Welcome to Civil Design Mastery! This lesson introduces you to the basics of civil design, its purpose, and its importance in construction projects.

Definition

Civil Design is the process of planning, analyzing, and detailing structures to ensure they are safe, durable, and functional. It covers structural elements like beams, slabs, columns, and foundations, ensuring they withstand all expected loads during their lifetime.

Thumb Rules

  • Always visualize the load path before designing.
  • Apply a safety factor of 1.5–2 for general structures.
  • Refer to local and international codes (Dubai GVIT, IS, BS).
  • Sketch simple diagrams to understand tension and compression zones.

Example

Example: Designing a simply supported beam for a residential floor:

  • Dead Load (self-weight) = 2 kN/m²
  • Live Load (people, furniture) = 3 kN/m²
  • Total Load = 5 kN/m² → Use for bending and shear calculations
