L1: Stress & Strain Basics

← Back to Phase 1

1) Definition

Stress (σ): Internal resistance force per unit area.

Strain (ε): Deformation per unit original length.

Stress unit is MPa (N/mm²). Strain is dimensionless (no unit).

2) Core formulas

σ = P / A
ε = ΔL / L
E = σ / ε

3) Examples

Example A: Load P = 100,000 N, area A = 500 mm².

σ = 100,000 / 500 = 200 N/mm² = 200 MPa

Example B: Bar length L = 2000 mm, extension ΔL = 1 mm.

ε = 1 / 2000 = 0.0005

4) Thumb rules

  • Higher area → lower stress for same load.
  • Steel E ≈ 200,000 MPa (very stiff).
  • Concrete E is much lower than steel.
  • Keep units consistent (N with mm², kN with m², etc.).

5) Practice exercise

Try this manually: P = 60 kN, A = 300 mm². Find stress in MPa.

Answer: 200 MPa.
