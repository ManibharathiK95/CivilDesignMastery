Units, Standards & Codes

In civil design, it is critical to use proper units and adhere to recognized standards and codes. Incorrect units or codes can lead to unsafe or non-compliant structures.

SI & Imperial Units

Quantity SI Unit Imperial Unit
Length Meter (m) Foot (ft)
Force Newton (N) Pound-force (lbf)
Stress Pascal (Pa) psi
Load kN/m² psf

Figure: Unit conversion flowchart (Placeholder)

Standards & Codes

  • IS Codes (India) – e.g., IS 456: 2000 for concrete, IS 800:2007 for steel
  • BS (British Standards)
  • ASTM (American Standards)
  • Dubai GVIT guidelines – mandatory for Dubai civil design licensing

Figure: Code → Design → Verification flowchart (Placeholder)

Example

Example: Designing a beam for a Dubai residential building:

  • Live load = 3 kN/m² (as per code)
  • Dead load = 2 kN/m²
  • Total design load = 5 kN/m² → Apply IS/BS/Dubai GVIT formulas
