Foundation Types

Foundations transfer building loads to the soil safely. Choosing the right type depends on soil conditions, building loads, and structural design.

Shallow Foundations

Shallow foundations are used where soil has adequate bearing capacity near the surface.

  • Isolated footing – for single columns
  • Strip footing – for walls
  • Raft foundation – entire floor slab acts as foundation

Deep Foundations

Deep foundations are used when surface soil is weak. Loads are transferred to deeper, stronger strata.

  • Pile foundation – concrete/steel piles driven into soil
  • Drilled shafts / caissons

Bearing Capacity & Settlement

Every foundation must support loads without excessive settlement. Bearing capacity is the maximum soil pressure it can safely support.

Example

A column with 200 kN load is supported on isolated footing. Soil bearing capacity = 150 kN/m². Required footing area = 200 / 150 ≈ 1.33 m²
