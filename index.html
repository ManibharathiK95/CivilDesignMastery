🔷 Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6)

L1: Stress & Strain Basics

What is Stress? Stress is internal force per unit area.

Formula: σ = P/A (Stress = Force / Area)

Example: If you apply 100 kN force on a column of 200mm × 200mm:
Area = 200×200 = 40,000 mm² = 0.04 m²
Stress = 100,000 N / 0.04 m² = 2,500,000 Pa = 2.5 MPa

What is Strain? Strain is deformation per unit length.

Formula: ε = δ/L (Strain = Change in length / Original length)

Example: A rod of length 2m stretches by 2mm:
Strain = 2mm / 2000mm = 0.001 (or 0.1%)

Hooke's Law: E = σ/ε (Young's Modulus)

For steel E ≈ 200 GPa, For concrete E ≈ 25-30 GPa

📌 Thumb Rules:
• Concrete compressive strength f'c = 25-40 MPa typical
• Steel yield strength fy = 420 or 500 MPa
• Normal stress units: MPa (N/mm²) or kPa (kN/m²)
💡 Key Concepts:
• Stress causes strain (more stress = more strain)
• Elastic limit: material returns to original shape
• Yield point: permanent deformation starts
• Ultimate strength: maximum stress before failure
🔢 Formulas to Remember Always:
• σ = P/A (Axial Stress)
• ε = δ/L (Strain)
• E = σ/ε (Hooke's Law)
• δ = PL/AE (Elongation)

L2: Bending Moment & Shear Force Diagrams

Shear Force (V): Force acting perpendicular to beam axis (tries to slide one part past another).

Bending Moment (M): Internal moment causing beam to bend.

Example - Simply Supported Beam with UDL:
Span L=6m, Load w=10 kN/m
• Reaction at each support = wL/2 = 10×6/2 = 30 kN
• Max Shear at support = 30 kN
• Max Moment at midspan = wL²/8 = 10×36/8 = 45 kNm

Relationship: dV/dx = -w (load), dM/dx = V (shear)

📌 Thumb Rules:
• SFD jumps at point loads (jump = load magnitude)
• SFD slopes under UDL (slope = -w)
• BMD max where V=0 (zero shear point)
• For cantilever: Max M at fixed end = wL²/2
🔢 Formulas:
• Simply supported + UDL: M_max = wL²/8, V_max = wL/2
• Simply supported + Point load P at center: M_max = PL/4
• Cantilever + UDL: M_max = wL²/2 (at fixed end)

L3: Bending Stress in Beams

Flexure Formula: σ = My/I

Where: M=Bending Moment, y=distance from neutral axis, I=Moment of Inertia

Example: Beam 300mm wide × 600mm deep, M=50 kNm, find max stress:
I = bd³/12 = 300×(600)³/12 = 5.4×10⁹ mm⁴
y_max = 300mm (half depth)
σ_max = (50×10⁶ Nmm)(300mm)/(5.4×10⁹ mm⁴) = 2.78 MPa

Section Modulus Z = I/y_max (simpler formula: σ = M/Z)

🔢 Formulas:
• σ = My/I (Bending Stress)
• σ = M/Z (Using Section Modulus)
• I_rectangular = bh³/12
• Z_rectangular = bh²/6

L4: Reinforced Concrete Beam Design (ACI 318)

Design Steps for Singly Reinforced Beam:

1. Calculate factored moment: Mu = 1.2DL + 1.6LL

2. Assume beam dimensions (b, h) and effective depth d

3. Calculate required steel ratio: ρ

4. Find steel area: As = ρ × b × d

5. Select bar size and number of bars

6. Check: ρ_min ≤ ρ ≤ ρ_max

Example: Mu = 150 kNm, b=300mm, d=500mm, f'c=28MPa, fy=420MPa
Rn = Mu/(φbd²) = (150×10⁶)/(0.9×300×500²) = 2.22 MPa
ρ ≈ 0.85(f'c/fy)[1-√(1-2Rn/(0.85f'c))] = 0.0055
As = 0.0055 × 300 × 500 = 825 mm²
Use 3#25 bars (As=1539mm²) ✓

📌 Thumb Rules:
• φ (phi) = 0.9 for flexure, 0.75 for shear
• ρ_min = 0.25√(f'c)/fy (but not less than 1.4/fy)
• ρ_max = 0.75ρb (for tension-controlled sections)
• Typical d = h - 65mm (cover+stirrup+half bar)
🔢 ACI 318 Key Formulas:
• φMn ≥ Mu (Design requirement)
• ρ = As/(bd) (Reinforcement ratio)
• ρb = 0.85β₁(f'c/fy)[87000/(87000+fy)]
• ld = (fyψtψe)/(25λ√f'c) × db (Development length)

L5: Post-Tensioning Fundamentals

What is Post-Tensioning? Applying compression to concrete AFTER it hardens using high-strength steel strands/tendons.

Why use PT? • Longer spans • Thinner slabs • Less cracking • Control deflections

Key Components:
• Strand: 0.5" diameter (12.7mm), Grade 270 (fpu=1860 MPa)
• Anchorage: Live end (stressing side) & Dead end (fixed side)
• Duct/sheathing: Protects strand, allows movement during stressing
• Grout: Cementitious fill after stressing to protect against corrosion

Prestress Force Example:
6 strands of 0.5", jacking stress = 0.75fpu = 1395 MPa
Area per strand = 93 mm², Total Ap = 6×93 = 558 mm²
Initial force Pi = 558 × 1395 = 778 kN

📌 PT Thumb Rules:
• Typical jacking stress: 0.70-0.80 fpu
• Effective stress after losses: ~0.55-0.65 fpu
• Total losses: 20-30% of initial force
• Slab span/depth ratio: 35-45 for PT vs 20-25 for RC
🔢 PT Basic Formulas:
• P = Aps × fpe (Effective prestress force)
• Eccentricity e = distance from CG of section to tendon CG
• Moment from PT: M_pt = P × e

L6: Losses in Post-Tensioning (CRITICAL TOPIC)

You MUST know all 6 types of losses for interviews!

INSTANTANEOUS LOSSES (happen immediately during stressing):

1. Anchorage Seating (Wedge Draw-in): Wedge pulls back ~6mm when jack released.
Formula: ΔP = (Ap × Ep × ΔL) / L
Example: ΔL=6mm, Ep=195000MPa, Ap=93mm², L=10m → ΔP = 10.9 kN per strand

2. Friction Loss: Two parts - Wobble (K) + Curvature (μ)
Formula: Px = P₀ × e^(-(Kx + μα))
K = 0.002/m (wobble), μ = 0.20 (curvature friction), α = angle change in radians

3. Elastic Shortening: Concrete compresses when PT applied, causing strand shortening.
Approximate: Δfes ≈ n × fci × (Ap/Ac) where n = Es/Ec

TIME-DEPENDENT LOSSES (happen over months/years):

4. Creep: Concrete deforms under sustained load (~5% loss)
5. Shrinkage: Concrete volume decreases as water leaves (~6% loss)
6. Relaxation: Steel stress reduces under constant strain (~3-4% loss)

Total Loss Estimate: ~20-30% of jacking force for building slabs

📌 Interview Tip:
Interviewers WILL ask "What are the losses?"
Memorize: A-E-C-S-R (Anchorage, Elastic, Creep, Shrinkage, Relaxation) + Friction
🔢 All Loss Formulas:
• Friction: Px = P₀ × e^(-(Kx+μα))
• Anchorage: ΔP = (Ap×Ep×ΔL)/L
• Elastic Shortening: Δf = n×fci×ρ
• Creep: Δfc = n×fci×Ψ×ρ (Ψ≈2.0)
• Shrinkage: Δfsh = εsh×Es (εsh≈400-800×10⁻⁶)
• Relaxation: ~2-4% of initial stress

L7: Load Balancing Method (Core PT Concept)

This is THE most important concept in PT design!

Idea: Parabolic tendon creates UPWARD force that balances DOWNWARD gravity load.

Upward Force Formula: w_balanced = 8Pe/L²

Where: P=prestress force, e=drape (eccentricity), L=span

Example: Span L=10m, P=1000kN, e(drape)=150mm=0.15m
w_balanced = 8 × 1000 × 0.15 / (10)² = 12 kN/m

If your Dead Load = 10 kN/m and Live Load = 5 kN/m:
• Balance 100% DL → Slab sees only LL (very efficient!)
• Or balance DL + part of LL for more conservative design

Unbalanced Moment = Moment from (Total Load - Balanced Load)

📌 Design Strategy:
• More P or more e → more balanced load → thinner slab possible
• But: limited by cover requirements and concrete stresses at transfer
• Typical balance: 65-85% of total service load
🔢 Must Remember:
• w_b = 8Pe/L² (Parabolic profile upward force)
• Pe = effective prestress after losses
• e = total drape (high point to low point distance)

L8: PT Slab Stress Checks (ACI 18.4)

At Transfer (Initial Stage):

• Top fiber: ≤ 0.25√f'ci (compression allowed)
• Bottom fiber: ≤ 0.6√f'ci (TENSION allowed! This is special for PT)

At Service (Long-term):

• Top fiber: ≤ 0.45√f'c (compression)
• Bottom fiber: Decompression allowed (no tension, but can be zero)

Example Check: f'ci=25MPa at transfer, bottom fiber stress calculated = 2.5 MPa (tension)
Allowable = 0.6√25 = 0.6×5 = 3.0 MPa
2.5 < 3.0 → ✅ PASS

📌 Why allow tension in PT?
Because prestress keeps cracks closed even if theoretical tension exists!
But: You MUST provide minimum bonded reinforcement (rebar) in tension zones.
🔢 ACI 18.4 Limits Summary:
• Transfer top: 0.25√f'ci (C)
• Transfer bottom: 0.6√f'ci (T)
• Service top: 0.45√f'c (C)
• Service bottom: 0 (decompression) or small tension with rebar

L9: Punching Shear Check (Flat Slabs)

Critical for flat plates! Columns can punch through slab.

Critical Section: At d/2 from face of column (d = effective depth)

Check: φVc ≥ Vu (factored shear force)

Vc Calculation (ACI 22.6.5):
Vc = factor × √f'c × b₀ × d
Where b₀ = perimeter of critical section

Example: Column 500×500mm, slab d=190mm, Vu=400kN, f'c=35MPa
Critical perimeter b₀ = 4×(500+d) = 4×690 = 2760mm
Vc = 0.17×√35×2760×190 = 527,000 N = 527 kN
φVc = 0.75×527 = 395 kN < Vu=400kN → ⚠️ MARGINAL (add shear reinforcement or increase thickness)

📌 Punching Shear Tips:
• Openings near columns REDUCE b₀ significantly
• Edge/corner columns have lower capacity (less perimeter)
• Solutions: Drop panels, column capitals, stud rails, thicker slab

L10: Column Design Basics

Short Column (Axial Only):

φPn = 0.65[0.85f'c(Ag-As) + fyAs]

Example: Column 600×600mm, f'c=40MPa, fy=500MPa, Pu=5000kN, use 2% steel
Ag = 360000 mm², As = 0.02×360000 = 7200 mm²
φPn = 0.65[0.85×40×(360000-7200) + 500×7200]
φPn = 0.65[11,953,600 + 3,600,000] = 10,110 kN > 5000kN ✅

Ties Spacing:
• Min 16×bar diameter OR 48×tie diameter OR least column dimension
• Max spacing: 0.75×column dimension (seismic zones stricter)

📌 Column Rules:
• Min reinforcement: 1% of Ag
• Max reinforcement: 8% of Ag (practical max: 4%)
• Clear cover: 40mm (interior), 50mm (exposure)
• Min 4 bars for rectangular, 6 for circular
