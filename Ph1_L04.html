Load Calculations

Load calculation is essential to ensure a structure can safely support all expected loads. These include dead, live, wind, seismic, and snow loads.

Types of Loads

  • Dead Load: Weight of structural elements themselves (beams, slabs, walls).
  • Live Load: Occupancy, furniture, vehicles, people.
  • Wind Load: Horizontal forces acting on building surfaces.
  • Seismic Load: Earthquake forces based on site location.
  • Snow Load: Weight of accumulated snow on roof (if applicable).

Example

Example: A floor has dead load = 2 kN/m², live load = 3 kN/m². Total load for design = 1.2*2 + 1.5*3 = 6.9 kN/m²
