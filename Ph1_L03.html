Basic Structural Concepts

In this lesson, we explore fundamental structural concepts: stress, strain, bending, shear, torsion, and load paths. Understanding these is essential before designing any structural element.

Stress & Strain

Stress is the internal resistance of a material to deformation under load. Strain is the measure of deformation.

  • Axial Stress = Force / Area
  • Shear Stress = Force / Area (parallel)
  • Strain = ΔLength / Original Length

Bending, Shear & Torsion

Beams and columns experience bending, shear, and torsion depending on load and support conditions.

Elastic vs Plastic Behavior

  • Elastic: Material returns to original shape after load removal.
  • Plastic: Material permanently deforms after yield stress.

Modulus of Elasticity & Poisson’s Ratio

Elastic modulus (E) = Stress / Strain. Poisson's ratio (ν) = Lateral strain / Axial strain.
