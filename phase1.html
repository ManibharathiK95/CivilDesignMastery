Lesson 1: Stress & Strain Basics 🧱

- -
- 🎯 Today's Goal:
- Understand stress and strain from scratch. These are the FOUNDATION of ALL structural engineering. -
- -

💡 The Core Concept

- -

If you put 100kg on a thin stick → it breaks.

-

The SAME 100kg on a thick column → nothing happens.

-

WHY? Not total force — force per UNIT AREA.

- -
-

Same Load → Different Results

-
-
-
-

Thin stick ❌

-
-
-
-

Thick column ✅

-
-
-

Same 100kg → Different area = Different STRESS

-
- - -
-
📖 Definition: What is Stress & Strain?
-
-

STRESS (σ sigma):

-
    -
  • Internal resistance force per unit area
    • -
  • Unit: MPa (N/mm²) or kPa (kN/m²)
    • -
  • Symbol: σ (sigma)
    • -
- -
- -

STRAIN (ε epsilon):

-
    -
  • Deformation per unit length
    • -
  • NO UNITS! It's a ratio
    • -
  • Usually very small: 0.001 to 0.0005
    • -
-
-
- - -
-
FUNDAMENTAL FORMULA
-
σ = P ÷ A
-
───────────────
-
ε = δ ÷ L
-
───────────────
-
E = σ ÷ ε (Hooke's Law)
- -
-
📋 Material Values:
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
MaterialE (MPa)Strength
Steel200,000fy = 420-500 MPa
Concrete25,000-30,000f'c = 25-40 MPa
- E = Young's Modulus = Stiffness
- Higher E = Stiffer material -
-
-
- - -
-
✏️ Solved Examples
-
-
-

Example 1: Column Stress Calculation

- -
- GIVEN:
- • Force P = 500 kN = 500,000 N
- • Area A = 300mm × 300mm = 90,000 mm² -
- -
- SOLUTION:
- σ = P/A = 500,000 ÷ 90,000 = 5.56 MPa
- ✅ Every mm² carries 5.56 Newtons of force -
- -
- -
-

Example 2: Steel Rod Strain

- -
- GIVEN:
- • Length L = 2000mm
- • Elongation δ = 2mm
-
- -
- SOLUTION:
- ε = δ/L = 2/2000 = 0.001 (or 0.1%)
- ✅ Strain has NO units! -
-
-
-
-
- - -
-
📌 Thumb Rules
-
-
    -
  • ✅ 1 MPa = 1 N/mm²
    • -
  • ✅ Building column stress: 8-15 MPa (safe zone)
    • -
  • ✅ Concrete f'c = 25-40 MPa
    • -
  • ✅ Steel fy = 420 or 500 MPa
    • -
  • ⚠️ If stress > f'c or fy → FAILURE!
    • -

Lesson 2: Bending Moment & Shear Force 📉

- -
- 🎯 Goal:
- What happens INSIDE a beam when you apply load? Two things happen simultaneously. -
- -

💡 Two Forces Inside a Loaded Beam

- -
-
- 1️⃣ SHEAR FORCE (V)

- Think scissors cutting paper.
- One part slides down.
- Other part holds it up.

- 🔑 Max shear at SUPPORTS
- 🔑 Zero shear at CENTER -
-
- 2️⃣ BENDING MOMENT (M)

- Beam bends (smile shape 😊)
- Top: COMPRESSION (squeezed)
- Bottom: TENSION (stretched)

- 🔑 Max moment at CENTER
- 🔑 Zero moment at SUPPORTS -
-
- - -
-
🔢 Key Formulas
-
-
- SIMPLY SUPPORTED + UDL:
- ┌────────────────────────────┐
- R = wL/2 (each support reaction)
- V_max = wL/2 (at supports)
- M_max = wL²/8 (at center)
- └────────────────────────────┘

- CANTILEVER + UDL:
- V_max = wL (at fixed end)
- M_max = wL²/2 (at fixed end) -
-
-
- - -
-
✏️ Worked Example
-
-
-

Problem: Beam span L=6m, UDL w=10 kN/m

- -
- Step 1: Total Load = 10×6 = 60 kN
- Reaction each support = 60/2 = 30 kN ↑ -
-
- Step 2: V_max = 30 kN (at supports)
- M_max = 45 kNm (at midspan) -
-
-
-
- -
- 💡 Golden Rule:
- Moment is MAXIMUM where Shear = ZERO ← Memorize this! -

Lesson 3: Bending Stress in Beams 📏

- -
- 🎯 Big Question:
- We know bending moment M from Lesson 2. Now: What STRESS does it create? -
- -

🔄 Cross-Section Behavior

- -
-

Beam Under Bending:

-
-
COMPRESSION (↑ squeezed)
-
Cross-Section
-
TENSION (↓ stretched)
-
← Neutral Axis (no stress!) →
-
-
- -
    -
  • TOP fibers: Compression (pushed inward)
    • -
  • BOTTOM fibers: Tension (pulled apart)
    • -
  • MIDDLE line: NEUTRAL AXIS - no stress!
    • -
- - -
-
🔢 Flexure Formula
-
-
- ⭐ σ = M × y / I
- Or SIMPLER: σ = M / Z where Z = I/y_max

- For rectangle: Z = bh²/6
- For circle: Z = πD³/32 -
-
-
- - -
-
✏️ Complete Example
-
-
-

Given: b=300mm, h=600mm, M=50 kNm

- -
- I = 300×600³/12 = 5.4×10⁹ mm⁴
- y_max = 600/2 = 300mm
- σ = (50×10⁶)(300)/(5.4×10⁹) = 2.78 MPa -
-
-
-
- -
- 💡 Why RC Design Exists:

- Concrete WEAK in tension (~10% of compression strength)!
- Put STEEL REBAR at bottom!
- Concrete handles compression at top.
- That's REINFORCED Concrete! 🏗️ -

Lesson 4: RC Beam Design (ACI 318) 🏗️

- -
- 🎯 MOST IMPORTANT LESSON YET!
- This is what you'll do 90% of time as design engineer. -
- -

🧠 Design Philosophy

- -
-
- ⬆️ Increase Loads
- DL × 1.2
- LL × 1.6 -
-
- ⬇ Reduce Capacity
- φ = 0.90 (flexure)
- φ = 0.75 (shear) -
-
- -

φMn ≥ Mu (Capacity ≥ Demand)

- - -
-
📋 Complete Design Steps
-
-
-

GIVEN: Span 6m, DL=15kN/m, LL=20kN/m, f'c=28MPa, fy=420MPa, b=300mm

- -
- Self-wt = 0.3×0.6×25 = 4.5 kN/m
- Total DL = 19.5 kN/m
- Wu = 1.2(19.5)+1.6(20) = 55.4 kN/m
- Mu = 55.4×36/8 = 249.3 kNm -
- -
- d = 540mm (h=600mm, minus cover+stirrup+half bar) -
- -
- Rn = Mu/(φbd²) = 3.18 MPa
- ρ = 0.0082
- As = ρbd = 1328 mm²
- Use 4#25 bars (As=1964 mm²) ✅ -
- -
-

✅ PASS! Design is safe.

-
-
-
- - -
-
📘 ACI 318 Reference
-
-
- φ_flexure = 0.90 | φ_shear = 0.75 | φ_compression = 0.65

- U = 1.2D + 1.6L (most common)

- Development Length: ld ≈ 45db (for typical bar)

- Practical Tips:
- • Beam depth ≈ span/10 to span/12
- • Effective depth d ≈ h - 65mm
- • Min 2 bars in beam width -
-
-
- -
- 🎯 Interview Question:

- Q: "Why φ=0.9 for flexure but 0.75 for shear?"
- A: "Shear failure is SUDDEN & BRITTLE (no warning!). Flexure failure is DUCTILE (steel yields first = time to escape!)" -

Lesson 5: Shear Design in Beams ✂️

- -
- ⚠️ WARNING:
- Many beam failures are due to SHEAR, not flexure! And shear failure gives NO WARNING. -
- -

🛡 How We Prevent Failure

- -
-
- Vc = Concrete Capacity
- Vc ≈ 0.17√f'c × bw × d
- Depends on concrete strength & size -
-
- Vs = Stirrup Contribution
- Vs = Av × fy × d / s
- s = spacing between stirrups -
-
- -

Total capacity: φ(Vc + Vs) must be ≥ Vu

- - -
-
✏️ Worked Example
-
-
-

GIVEN: bw=300mm, d=540mm, f'c=28MPa, fy=420MPa, Vu=150kN

- -
- Vc = 0.17×√28×300×540 = 145.7 kN
- φVc = 0.75×145.7 = 109.3 kN
- Vu (150) > φVc (109.3)? YES → Need stirrups! -
- -
- Vs needed = 150/0.75 - 145.7 = 54.3 kN
- Use T10 2-legged stirrups: Av = 157 mm²
- s = (157×420×540)/54,300 = 658mm → Use s=250mm c/c -
-
-
-
- -
- 📌 Practical Rules:
- • First check: Is Vu ≤ φVc/2? → Minimum stirrups only
- • Always CLOSED LOOPS (not open)
- • Start 50mm from support face
- Common sizes: T8, T10, T12 -

Lesson 6: One-Way Slab Design 🏢

- -
- 🎯 Today:
- Slabs are simpler than beams (no stirrinks!), but understand the differences. -
- - -
-
📋 Slab vs Beam Differences
-
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
AspectBeamSlab
Width (b)Actual widthAlways 1000mm
ThicknessBy calculationBy span/depth ratio
StirrupsNOT REQUIRED
Extra RebarTemperature/Shrinkage rebar
-
-
- - -
-
✏️ Slab Design Example
-
-
-

GIVEN: Span=4m, LL=3kPa, Finish=1.5kPa, f'c=28MPa, fy=420MPa

- -
- Thickness: L/20 = 4000/20 = 200mm, d≈170mm -
- -
- Loads: Self-wt=5.0, Total DL=6.5, Wu=12.6 kN/m -
- -
- Moment: Mu = WL²/8 = 25.2 kNm/m
- As_main = 493mm²/m → T12@200 c/c
- As_temp = 360mm²/m → T10@200 c/c -
-
-
- -
- ✅ FINAL ANSWER:

- Main steel: T12@200 c/c (bottom)
- Distribution: T10@200 c/c (perpendicular)
- Thickness: 200mm -
- - -
-
📋 Cheat Sheet
-
-
- Thickness: SS=L/20, Cont=L/24, Both cont=L/28, Cantilever=L/10
- Temp rebar: As = 0.0018×b×h
- Max spacing: main=min(3h,450mm), temp=min(5h,450mm)
